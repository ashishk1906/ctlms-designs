Introduction - Numbers

Activity 1: Create 2 digit numbers using dice.

One/single digit number's place value is called as units/ones (Ex: Numbers from 0 to 9).

9 is the largest single digit number and when 1 is added to 9 + 1 = 10.

10 is the smallest double number.

Two/double digit number’s place value is called as tens. (Ex: In a number 18 - 1's place value is tens and 8's place value is units/ones).

99 is the largest double-digit number.

Activity 2: Create 10 bundles of ice cream sticks of 10 sticks in each bundle.

Eg: 9 bundles of ice cream sticks of 10 is equal to 90 (ninety).

9 bundles of ice cream sticks of 10 is 90 + 6 units = 96 (ninety six).

9 bundles of ice cream sticks of 10 is 90 + 9 units = 99 (ninety nine).

9 bundles of ice cream sticks of 10 is 90 + 10 units = 100 (one hundred).

3-digit number’s place value is called as hundreds. (Ex: In a number 264 - 2's place value is hundreds, 6's place value is tens and 4's place value is units/ones).
